Medicines Created with AI for Different Diseases

    Cancer Treatment:
  • AI-Designed Cancer Medicines: AI systems, such as Insilico Medicine, are being used to create drugs that target specific cancer mutations. For example, their AI platform helped develop a potential treatment for non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC), which is now being tested in clinical trials.
    • Diabetes:
  • AI for Insulin Control: AI-powered devices, like smart insulin pumps and continuous glucose monitors (CGMs) such as Medtronic's MiniMed™ system, help people with diabetes by automatically adjusting insulin based on their current glucose levels.
  • AI in Diabetes Drug Research: AI is also being used to find new treatments for type 2 diabetes. It helps predict how existing medications could be repurposed for better results.
    • Mental Health:
  • AI in Finding Antidepressants: AI is being used to predict which antidepressants and anxiety medications might work best. It’s also helping researchers find existing drugs that could be used for mental health conditions like depression, bipolar disorder, and schizophrenia.
  • AI for Personalized Treatment: AI platforms like Woebot are offering cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and other mental health support through chatbots, making care more accessible to people.
    • Cardiovascular Disease:
  • AI-Developed Heart Medications: AI technology is being used to find new medicines to treat heart problems, like high cholesterol or heart failure. For instance, AI simulations helped identify possible drugs to treat the buildup of plaque in the arteries, which is a common cause of heart attacks.
    • Antibiotic Resistance:
  • AI-Designed Antibiotics: AI systems, like those created by MIT’s Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL), are being used to find new antibiotics to fight bacteria that are resistant to traditional drugs. One AI system even discovered halicin, a promising antibiotic that works against tough bacteria like E. coli and tuberculosis.
